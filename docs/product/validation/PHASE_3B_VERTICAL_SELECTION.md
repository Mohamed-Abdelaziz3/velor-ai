# Phase 3B: Vertical Selection

## 1. Candidate Evaluation Scorecard

| Criterion | Office Furniture / Chairs | Local Apparel Brands | Home Products / Accessories |
| --------- | ------------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------- |
| **Average Order Value (AOV)** | High | Medium | Medium-Low |
| **Conversation Volume** | Medium | High | High |
| **Product/Catalog Complexity**| Low (few variants) | High (size, color, season) | Low-Medium (standardized) |
| **Price Stability** | High | Low (frequent sales) | High |
| **Repeated Questions** | High (specs, delivery) | High (sizing, fit) | High (dimensions, stock) |
| **Follow-up Dependence** | High (B2B or large B2C) | Low (impulse buy) | Medium |
| **Purchase Handoff** | Medium (invoice/link) | High (returns, size swap)| Low (simple cart link) |
| **Software Adoption Willingness**| Medium | Medium | Medium |
| **Ease of Recruitment** | Medium | High | High |
| **Support Burden** | Low | High (complex returns) | Medium |
| **Regulatory Risk** | None | None | None |

## 2. Recommendations

### **Primary Vertical: Office Furniture / Chairs**
- **Reasoning:** High AOV justifies the cost of a pilot product immediately. The catalog is highly structured with few variants (e.g., Ergo Pro vs. Ergo One). Purchases are deliberated, meaning follow-up and lead recovery have a massive financial impact. The workflow heavily depends on capturing the customer's budget and providing exact dimensions.
- **Assumptions to Validate:** Whether merchants in this vertical receive enough inbound volume to feel acute daily operational pain, and whether they are willing to share catalog truth.

### **Secondary Vertical: Home Products / Consumer Accessories**
- **Reasoning:** Higher conversation volume with moderate AOV. Products are relatively standardized (e.g., lamps, mirrors, electronics), making catalog input straightforward. Follow-up is still valuable but less critical than high-ticket furniture.
- **Assumptions to Validate:** Whether the pain of message volume outweighs the pain of lost individual sales.

### **Vertical to Defer: Local Apparel Brands**
- **Reasoning:** While they have massive message volume and acute pain, the catalog complexity is too high for the Thin Pilot. Sizing charts, stock-outs by size/color, frequent discounts, and high return/exchange rates introduce too much edge-case complexity for a V1 product focused on pure Conversation-to-Action.
