# Pilot Pricing Experiment

## 1. Philosophy
Pricing is an experiment to test willingness to pay and perceived value, not an immediate revenue generator. We test three distinct offers to gauge the friction of adoption versus the perceived value of the problem solved.

## 2. Offer A: Free Short Trial
- **Price:** 0 EGP for 7 days.
- **Included Scope:** Full Inbox access, up to 10 products in catalog.
- **Merchant Effort:** High. Merchant must manually input their own catalog using the new UI.
- **Success Condition:** Merchant actually spends the 30 minutes required to input data and routes real traffic.
- **Continuation:** At day 7, present standard pricing to keep the service active.
- **Real Commitment:** Providing actual operational data and time.

## 3. Offer B: Paid Pilot
- **Price:** 300–500 EGP for 14 days.
- **Included Scope:** Full Inbox access, up to 50 products.
- **Merchant Effort:** Medium. VELOR assists with initial catalog load.
- **Success Condition:** Merchant agrees to pay the fee upfront.
- **Continuation:** Converts to a monthly subscription of 1000 EGP.
- **Real Commitment:** Financial payment prior to seeing automated value.

## 4. Offer C: Assisted Pilot / Premium Setup
- **Price:** 750–1000 EGP setup + 14 day trial.
- **Included Scope:** Full white-glove setup. VELOR team scrapes their Instagram/FB to build the catalog entirely.
- **Merchant Effort:** Zero setup effort. Only daily usage required.
- **Success Condition:** Merchant pays the premium setup fee to avoid data-entry labor.
- **Continuation:** Converts to standard subscription.
- **Real Commitment:** High upfront payment to bypass operational friction.

## 5. Execution Rules
- Never promise "10x returns" or "guaranteed revenue".
- Frame the pricing interview around value: *"During the pilot, we saw 4 abandoned follow-ups converted into sales, representing roughly 4,000 EGP in recovered revenue. To keep the system running automatically for your entire catalog, the cost is X. Does this make commercial sense for your business?"*
